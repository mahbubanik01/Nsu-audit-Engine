# Program: Computer Science & Engineering

- Degree: Bachelor of Science (BS)
- Total Credits Required: 130
- Minimum CGPA: 2.0

## Program Overview
The Computer Science & Engineering (CSE) program is a rigorous 4-year undergraduate degree that provides comprehensive education in computing, software engineering, and computer systems. The program prepares students for careers in software development, systems engineering, and technology innovation.

## Mandatory GED Courses (12 Credits)
All students must complete:
- ENG102 (Composition)
- ENG103 (English Composition)
- HIS103 (Bangladesh Studies)
- PHI101 (Ethics and Philosophy)

## Core Mathematics (15 Credits - 5 Courses)
All students must complete the following mathematics courses:
- MAT116 (Pre-Calculus)
- MAT120 (Differential Calculus)
- MAT250 (Integral Calculus & Differential Equations)
- MAT350 (Discrete Mathematics)
- MAT361 (Linear Algebra & Statistics)

## Major Core Courses (33 Credits - 11 Courses)
All CSE students must complete:
- CSE115 (Programming Language I)
- CSE173 (Discrete Mathematics)
- CSE215 (Programming Language II)
- CSE225 (Data Structures)
- CSE231 (Digital Logic Design)
- CSE311 (Database Management Systems)
- CSE323 (Operating Systems)
- CSE327 (Software Engineering)
- CSE331 (Microprocessors & Assembly Language)
- CSE332 (Computer Organization & Architecture)
- CSE425 (Computer Networks)

## Physics Requirements (8 Credits)
- PHY107 (Physics I) - 3 credits
- PHY107L (Physics I Lab) - 1 credit
- PHY108 (Physics II) - 3 credits
- PHY108L (Physics II Lab) - 1 credit

## Additional Requirements
Students must complete additional courses to reach 130 total credits, including:
- CSE electives (minimum 6 courses)
- General electives
- Technical electives

## Special Requirements

### Graduation Requirements
To graduate with a BS in CSE, students must:
1. Complete at least 130 credits
2. Achieve a minimum CGPA of 2.0
3. Complete all mandatory GED courses
4. Complete all core mathematics courses
5. Complete all major core courses
6. Complete the capstone project (CSE499)

### Academic Standing
- **First Class**: CGPA 3.00 and above
- **Second Class**: CGPA 2.50 to 2.99
- **Third Class**: CGPA 2.00 to 2.49
- **Academic Probation**: CGPA below 2.00

## Retake Policy
- Students may retake any course with a grade of C+ or lower
- Only the best grade counts toward CGPA
- All attempts appear on the transcript
