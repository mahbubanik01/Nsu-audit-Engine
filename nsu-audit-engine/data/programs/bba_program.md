# Program: Business Administration

- Degree: Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA)
- Total Credits Required: 126
- Minimum CGPA: 2.0

## Program Overview
The BBA program at NSU is a comprehensive 4-year undergraduate degree designed to prepare students for careers in business and entrepreneurship. The program follows a trimester system (Spring, Summer, Fall) and requires completion of 126 credits across general education, core business courses, and specialized major courses.

## Waivable Courses
The following courses can be waived based on admission test performance:
- ENG102 (Elementary English)
- BUS112 (Elementary Mathematics)

**Note**: If waived, these courses do not need to be completed and do not count toward the 126-credit requirement.

- BUS173 (Business Statistics II)

- ACT202 (Principles of Accounting II)
- FIN254 (Corporate Finance)
- LAW200 (Business Law)
- INB372 (International Business)
- MKT202 (Principles of Marketing)
- MIS207 (Management Information Systems)
- MGT212 (Principles of Management)
- MGT351 (Operations Management)
- MGT314 (Human Resource Management)
- MGT368 (Organizational Behavior)
- MGT489 (Strategic Management)

## General Education (GED) Courses (36 Credits - 12 Courses)

### Mandatory GED Courses (12 Credits)
- ENG103 (English Composition)
- ENG105 (Public Speaking & Presentation)
- PHI401 (Business Ethics)
- HIS103 (Bangladesh Studies)

### History Elective (Choose 1 of 4)
Select one course (3 credits):
- HIS101 (History of the Emergence of Independent Bangladesh)
- HIS102 (History and Culture of Bangladesh)
- HIS202 (World Civilizations)
- HIS205 (Economic History of Bangladesh)

### Political Science/Public Administration (Choose 1 of 3)
Select one course (3 credits):
- POL101 (Introduction to Political Science)
- POL104 (Introduction to Public Administration)
- PAD201 (Governance and Development in Bangladesh)

### Social Science Elective (Choose 1 of 3)
Select one course (3 credits):
- SOC101 (Introduction to Sociology)
- GEO205 (Geography of Bangladesh)
- ANT101 (Introduction to Anthropology)

### Basic Sciences (Choose 3 of 6)
Select three courses (9 credits total) from:
- BIO103 (Introduction to Biology)
- ENV107 (Environmental Science)
- PBH101 (Introduction to Public Health)
- PSY101 (Introduction to Psychology)
- PHY107 (Principles of Physics)
- CHE101 (Introduction to Chemistry)

**Note**: Each basic science course also requires a corresponding lab (1 credit each, total 3 lab credits).

## Major Courses (18 Credits - 6 Courses)
Students must declare a major and complete 18 credits in their chosen field:
- 4 Major Core Courses (12 credits) - Mandatory for the major
- 2 Major Elective Courses (6 credits) - Selected from major offerings

### Available Majors
- Marketing
- Finance
- Accounting
- Human Resource Management
- International Business
- Management
- MIS (Management Information Systems)
- Supply Chain Management
- Economics
- Entrepreneurship

## Free Electives (9 Credits - 3 Courses)
Students may select any 3 courses from any department to fulfill free elective requirements.

## Internship (4 Credits - Non-Counting)
- MGT499 (Internship) is required but does NOT count toward the 126-credit graduation requirement
- Can be taken after completing 60 credits
- Students may take up to 2 courses alongside internship

## Special Requirements

### Graduation Requirements
To graduate with a BBA degree, students must:
1. Complete at least 126 credits (excluding internship)
2. Achieve a minimum CGPA of 2.0
3. Complete all mandatory courses
4. Satisfy all course group requirements
5. Complete all major requirements

### Academic Standing
- **First Class**: CGPA 3.00 and above
- **Second Class**: CGPA 2.50 to 2.99
- **Third Class**: CGPA 2.00 to 2.49
- **Academic Probation**: CGPA below 2.00

### Honors
- **Summa Cum Laude**: CGPA 3.90 - 4.00
- **Magna Cum Laude**: CGPA 3.80 - 3.89
- **Cum Laude**: CGPA 3.50 - 3.79

## Retake Policy
- Students may retake any course with a grade of B or lower
- When a course is retaken, both grades appear on the transcript
- Only the best grade is used to calculate CGPA
- F grades count as 0.0 in CGPA until the course is retaken and passed

## Notes for Administrators
- This curriculum applies to students enrolled Fall 2014 (ID starting with 143) onwards
- Transfer students must complete at least 50% of core courses at NSU
- International students must meet additional English proficiency requirements
- For dual majors, total credits increase to 135 (9 additional major courses replace free electives)
